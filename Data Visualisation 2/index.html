Introduction

The Olympics, otherwise known as the Olympic Games, are international sporting events that include both Summer and Winter sports. The first Olympic Games was held on 6 April 1896 in Athens, and the most recent Olympic Games was the postponed 2020 Summer Olympics held on 23 July 2021 in Tokyo.

Thousands of athletes from over 200 nations participate in the Olympics, which is typically held every 4 years, alternating between Summer and Winter Olympics every 2 years. Before the 1992 Olympic Games, they were held every 4 years, with no distinction between Summer and Winter sports.

Due to World War 1, there were no Olympic Games held between 1912 - 1920, and due to World War 2, there were no Olympic Games held between 1936 - 1948.
